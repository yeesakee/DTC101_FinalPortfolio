Class Reflection

 Throughout this course, I have gathered significant information and made noteworthy observations. One crucial aspect I have learned is the need for mindfulness regarding the online content we consume. It is essential to recognize that data is inherently biased since it originates from human sources. Each individual possesses their own beliefs and biases, meaning that our data can never truly be devoid of such influences.
 Moreover, the value and significance of our personal data cannot be overstated. Although safeguarding our data can be challenging, it is crucial to exercise caution and mindfulness when choosing which organizations to entrust with our information. Additionally, we should remain aware of how easily personal data can be accessed by others, prompting us to be more discerning in our data-sharing practices.
 While curation serves as a useful tool for efficiently consuming information, it is important to approach curated articles and papers with a critical mindset. Often, such materials can be manipulated to align with the viewpoint and objectives of the author or organization behind them. This manipulation may involve misleading content intended to promote specific agendas or generate profit. It is vital for us to fact-check the articles and information we encounter online.
 The concept of copyright plays a crucial role in safeguarding creators from exploitation. However, determining what creations should be protected by copyright can be challenging due to their diverse nature. Balancing the need for protection with encouraging innovation and creativity remains an ongoing challenge in copyright law.
 Another significant observation is the tendency to embrace the narrative of "newer is better" or "older is better." Rather than subscribing to these dichotomies, it is more beneficial to appreciate the potential for learning and improvement from both newer and older iterations. Restricting ourselves to the notion of superiority hinders progress, impeding our ability to create better and more innovative versions.
 Finally, I have come to understand that design is a multifaceted process involving idea generation, multiple prototyping iterations, and rigorous testing. Each step in this process contributes to the identification and creation of the most effective design that aligns with our original concept or addresses the problem at hand. Through this iterative approach, we can continually refine our designs and achieve optimal outcomes.
